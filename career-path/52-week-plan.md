# 52-Week Staff Engineer Development Plan

**Goal**: Senior Engineer → Staff Engineer  
**Timeline**: 12 months (May 4, 2026 - May 3, 2027)  
**Path**: Staff → EM → Senior EM → Director of Engineering

---

## Q1: Technical Foundation (Weeks 1-13)

Build deep understanding of production systems and architecture patterns. This quarter establishes the technical credibility that bootcamp grads often lack.

### Week 1 — May 4, 2026
- [ ] **Goal**: Identify 3 critical production systems at your company to study
- **Why**: Staff engineers understand how systems work across the org, not just their team
- **Done when**: You have a list of 3 systems with their owners and a brief description of what each does

### Week 2 — May 11, 2026
- [ ] **Goal**: Map the architecture of System #1 (draw boxes and arrows)
- **Why**: Visual understanding reveals dependencies, bottlenecks, and design decisions
- **Done when**: You have a diagram showing components, data flow, and external dependencies

### Week 3 — May 18, 2026
- [ ] **Goal**: Read the code for System #1's critical path (the main request flow)
- **Why**: Understanding implementation details lets you evaluate tradeoffs, not just accept them
- **Done when**: You can explain what happens from request to response in 5 minutes

### Week 4 — May 25, 2026
- [ ] **Goal**: Interview the owner of System #1: "Why did you choose X over Y?"
- **Why**: Learning the rejected alternatives teaches you more than the chosen solution
- **Done when**: You've documented 3+ design decisions and their tradeoffs

### Week 5 — Jun 1, 2026
- [ ] **Goal**: Map the architecture of System #2
- **Why**: Comparing systems reveals patterns and anti-patterns
- **Done when**: You have a diagram comparable to Week 2

### Week 6 — Jun 8, 2026
- [ ] **Goal**: Read the code for System #2's critical path
- **Why**: Different systems make different tradeoffs - understanding why builds judgment
- **Done when**: You can explain the request flow and compare it to System #1

### Week 7 — Jun 15, 2026
- [ ] **Goal**: Interview the owner of System #2 about design decisions
- **Why**: Different engineers have different philosophies - exposure builds your own
- **Done when**: You've documented 3+ design decisions and their tradeoffs

### Week 8 — Jun 22, 2026
- [ ] **Goal**: Map the architecture of System #3
- **Why**: Three systems gives you enough data to see patterns
- **Done when**: You have a diagram comparable to Weeks 2 and 5

### Week 9 — Jun 29, 2026
- [ ] **Goal**: Read the code for System #3's critical path
- **Why**: By now you should be faster at understanding new systems
- **Done when**: You can explain the request flow and compare it to Systems #1 and #2

### Week 10 — Jul 6, 2026
- [ ] **Goal**: Interview the owner of System #3 about design decisions
- **Why**: You're building a network of technical relationships
- **Done when**: You've documented 3+ design decisions and their tradeoffs

### Week 11 — Jul 13, 2026
- [ ] **Goal**: Write a comparison doc: "Architecture Patterns at [Company]"
- **Why**: Synthesizing knowledge into writing forces clarity and creates a shareable artifact
- **Done when**: You have a 2-3 page doc comparing how the 3 systems handle common concerns (scaling, reliability, data consistency)

### Week 12 — Jul 20, 2026
- [ ] **Goal**: Share your comparison doc with the system owners for feedback
- **Why**: Getting feedback from Staff+ engineers builds relationships and improves your understanding
- **Done when**: You've received feedback from at least 2 of the 3 owners

### Week 13 — Jul 27, 2026
- [ ] **Goal**: Present your findings to your team (15-minute tech talk)
- **Why**: Teaching solidifies learning and establishes you as someone who understands systems
- **Done when**: You've delivered the presentation and answered questions

## Q2: Cross-Team Technical Impact (Weeks 14-26)

Move from understanding systems to influencing them. Staff engineers don't just know how things work - they make them better.

### Week 14 — Aug 3, 2026
- [ ] **Goal**: Identify a cross-team technical problem that needs solving
- **Why**: Staff-level work spans team boundaries
- **Done when**: You have a problem statement and a list of affected teams (minimum 2)

### Week 15 — Aug 10, 2026
- [ ] **Goal**: Write a 1-page problem statement and share with your manager
- **Why**: Getting buy-in early prevents wasted effort
- **Done when**: Your manager agrees this is worth pursuing

### Week 16 — Aug 17, 2026
- [ ] **Goal**: Interview engineers from each affected team about the problem
- **Why**: Understanding different perspectives prevents solutions that work for one team but break another
- **Done when**: You've talked to at least one engineer from each affected team

### Week 17 — Aug 24, 2026
- [ ] **Goal**: Research how other companies solved similar problems
- **Why**: You don't need to invent everything - learning from others is efficient
- **Done when**: You have 3+ examples of how other companies approached this

### Week 18 — Aug 31, 2026
- [ ] **Goal**: Draft 2-3 potential solutions with tradeoffs
- **Why**: Staff engineers present options, not just recommendations
- **Done when**: You have a doc with multiple approaches and their pros/cons

### Week 19 — Sep 7, 2026
- [ ] **Goal**: Get feedback on your solutions from a Staff+ engineer
- **Why**: Early feedback from senior engineers catches blind spots
- **Done when**: You've incorporated their feedback into your doc

### Week 20 — Sep 14, 2026
- [ ] **Goal**: Write a full design doc for your recommended solution
- **Why**: Design docs are the primary artifact of Staff-level work
- **Done when**: You have a complete design doc following your company's template

### Week 21 — Sep 21, 2026
- [ ] **Goal**: Present your design doc in a cross-team review
- **Why**: Defending your design builds credibility and improves the solution
- **Done when**: You've presented and captured feedback/action items

### Week 22 — Sep 28, 2026
- [ ] **Goal**: Revise your design based on review feedback
- **Why**: Incorporating feedback shows you can collaborate, not just dictate
- **Done when**: Your design doc is updated and approved

### Week 23 — Oct 5, 2026
- [ ] **Goal**: Create an implementation plan with milestones
- **Why**: Staff engineers think about execution, not just design
- **Done when**: You have a phased plan with clear deliverables

### Week 24 — Oct 12, 2026
- [ ] **Goal**: Begin implementation (or coordinate with teams who will implement)
- **Why**: Designs that don't ship don't count
- **Done when**: Work has started on the first milestone

### Week 25 — Oct 19, 2026
- [ ] **Goal**: Write a progress update and share with stakeholders
- **Why**: Communication is part of the job - keeping people informed builds trust
- **Done when**: You've sent an update to all affected teams

### Week 26 — Oct 26, 2026
- [ ] **Goal**: Retrospective: What worked? What would you do differently?
- **Why**: Reflection accelerates learning
- **Done when**: You've written a personal retrospective (doesn't need to be shared)

## Q3: Technical Authority (Weeks 27-39)

Establish yourself as the go-to person for specific technical domains. Staff engineers are known for something.

### Week 27 — Nov 2, 2026
- [ ] **Goal**: Identify your technical domain (what do you want to be known for?)
- **Why**: Staff engineers have depth, not just breadth
- **Done when**: You've chosen a domain (e.g., "observability", "data pipelines", "API design")

### Week 28 — Nov 9, 2026
- [ ] **Goal**: Audit your company's current state in your chosen domain
- **Why**: You can't improve what you don't understand
- **Done when**: You have a doc listing current tools, practices, and pain points

### Week 29 — Nov 16, 2026
- [ ] **Goal**: Research industry best practices in your domain
- **Why**: Knowing what "good" looks like lets you set direction
- **Done when**: You have notes on 5+ external resources (blog posts, talks, papers)

### Week 30 — Nov 23, 2026
- [ ] **Goal**: Write a "State of [Domain] at [Company]" doc
- **Why**: This establishes you as someone who thinks strategically about this area
- **Done when**: You have a doc with current state, gaps, and opportunities

### Week 31 — Nov 30, 2026
- [ ] **Goal**: Share your doc with your manager and get feedback
- **Why**: Alignment with leadership prevents wasted effort
- **Done when**: Your manager has reviewed and you've incorporated feedback

### Week 32 — Dec 7, 2026
- [ ] **Goal**: Identify one quick win you can ship in your domain
- **Why**: Credibility comes from shipping, not just writing docs
- **Done when**: You have a scoped improvement you can complete in 2-3 weeks

### Week 33 — Dec 14, 2026
- [ ] **Goal**: Ship your quick win
- **Why**: Momentum matters - early wins build credibility for bigger changes
- **Done when**: The improvement is deployed and working

### Week 34 — Dec 21, 2026
- [ ] **Goal**: Write a brief case study of your quick win
- **Why**: Documenting impact makes it visible and referenceable
- **Done when**: You have a 1-page doc with problem, solution, and results

### Week 35 — Dec 28, 2026
- [ ] **Goal**: Mentor a junior engineer on something in your domain
- **Why**: Teaching establishes authority and multiplies your impact
- **Done when**: You've had at least 2 mentoring sessions

### Week 36 — Jan 4, 2027
- [ ] **Goal**: Attend or present at an architecture review outside your team
- **Why**: Visibility across the org is essential for Staff promotion
- **Done when**: You've participated in a review and contributed meaningfully

### Week 37 — Jan 11, 2027
- [ ] **Goal**: Write a technical blog post or internal doc about your domain
- **Why**: Written artifacts scale your influence beyond 1:1 conversations
- **Done when**: You've published something others can reference

### Week 38 — Jan 18, 2027
- [ ] **Goal**: Propose a larger initiative in your domain (3-6 month scope)
- **Why**: Staff engineers drive multi-quarter technical strategy
- **Done when**: You have a proposal doc shared with your manager

### Week 39 — Jan 25, 2027
- [ ] **Goal**: Get buy-in for your larger initiative
- **Why**: Influence without authority means convincing, not commanding
- **Done when**: Your manager and at least one other stakeholder support the initiative

## Q4: Staff Promotion Case (Weeks 40-52)

Build the evidence for your promotion. Staff promotions require documented impact across teams.

### Week 40 — Feb 1, 2027
- [ ] **Goal**: Review your company's Staff Engineer expectations/rubric
- **Why**: You need to know exactly what you're being evaluated against
- **Done when**: You have a checklist of Staff-level expectations

### Week 41 — Feb 8, 2027
- [ ] **Goal**: Map your work from the past year to the Staff rubric
- **Why**: Identifying gaps now gives you time to fill them
- **Done when**: You have a doc showing which expectations you meet and which you don't

### Week 42 — Feb 15, 2027
- [ ] **Goal**: Identify 2-3 gaps and create a plan to address them
- **Why**: Proactive gap-filling shows self-awareness and initiative
- **Done when**: You have specific actions for each gap

### Week 43 — Feb 22, 2027
- [ ] **Goal**: Talk to a Staff+ engineer about their promotion experience
- **Why**: Learning from others who've done it reveals hidden requirements
- **Done when**: You've had a 30+ minute conversation and taken notes

### Week 44 — Mar 1, 2027
- [ ] **Goal**: Start drafting your promotion packet (impact summary)
- **Why**: Writing it early gives you time to strengthen weak areas
- **Done when**: You have a first draft of your impact summary

### Week 45 — Mar 8, 2027
- [ ] **Goal**: Gather metrics and evidence for your impact claims
- **Why**: "I improved performance" is weak; "I reduced p99 latency by 40%" is strong
- **Done when**: Each impact claim has supporting data

### Week 46 — Mar 15, 2027
- [ ] **Goal**: Get feedback on your draft from your manager
- **Why**: Your manager will present your case - they need to believe it
- **Done when**: You've incorporated their feedback

### Week 47 — Mar 22, 2027
- [ ] **Goal**: Identify 3-5 people who can speak to your Staff-level work
- **Why**: Peer feedback is part of most promotion processes
- **Done when**: You have a list of people and what they can speak to

### Week 48 — Mar 29, 2027
- [ ] **Goal**: Have informal conversations with your potential supporters
- **Why**: Giving them context helps them advocate for you effectively
- **Done when**: You've talked to at least 3 of them

### Week 49 — Apr 5, 2027
- [ ] **Goal**: Finalize your promotion packet
- **Why**: A polished packet makes your manager's job easier
- **Done when**: Your packet is complete and reviewed by your manager

### Week 50 — Apr 12, 2027
- [ ] **Goal**: Discuss promotion timeline with your manager
- **Why**: Understanding the process helps you plan
- **Done when**: You know when the next promotion cycle is and what to expect

### Week 51 — Apr 19, 2027
- [ ] **Goal**: Continue executing on your larger initiative from Q3
- **Why**: Ongoing impact matters - you can't coast after submitting your packet
- **Done when**: You've made visible progress this week

### Week 52 — Apr 26, 2027
- [ ] **Goal**: Reflect on the year and plan for Year 2
- **Why**: Whether you get promoted or not, you've grown significantly
- **Done when**: You've written a personal retrospective and identified focus areas for next year

## Tracking Your Progress

### Monthly Check-ins
At the end of each month, answer these questions:
1. What did I ship that had cross-team impact?
2. What technical decisions did I influence?
3. Who outside my team knows my work?
4. What would I do differently next month?

### Quarterly Reviews
At the end of each quarter, review:
1. Am I on track for Staff promotion?
2. What gaps remain?
3. What relationships have I built?
4. What artifacts (docs, code, designs) demonstrate my impact?

---

## Key Principles

**Ship over study**: Reading about system design is good. Designing and shipping systems is better.

**Artifacts over conversations**: Write things down. Design docs, architecture diagrams, and technical blog posts scale your influence.

**Cross-team over single-team**: Staff engineers are measured by org-wide impact, not team-level output.

**Tradeoffs over solutions**: Anyone can propose a solution. Staff engineers explain why it's the right tradeoff.

**Relationships over credentials**: Your CS degree (or lack thereof) matters less than whether Staff+ engineers respect your technical judgment.
