# Years 2-5: Staff Engineer to Director

**Timeline**: May 2027 - May 2031  
**Path**: Staff Engineer → Engineering Manager → Senior EM → Director

This is a loose roadmap. Reality will diverge. The value is having a direction, not a rigid plan.

---

## Year 2 (May 2027 - May 2028): Staff Engineer

**Status**: You've either been promoted to Staff or you're close.

### If Promoted to Staff

**Q1-Q2: Establish Staff-Level Operating Rhythm**
- Own a technical domain across multiple teams
- Lead 1-2 major technical initiatives per quarter
- Mentor 2-3 engineers (mix of junior and senior)
- Write 2-3 design docs that get implemented
- Present at company-wide engineering meetings

**Q3-Q4: Explore Management Interest**
- Shadow your EM in 1:1s, planning, and performance reviews (with permission)
- Take on "EM-adjacent" work: run team retros, lead hiring for your team
- Have explicit conversations with your manager about EM interest
- Talk to 3+ EMs about what they love and hate about the role

**Key Question**: Do you actually want to manage people, or do you like the idea of it?

### If Not Yet Promoted

- Double down on the gaps identified in Year 1
- Get explicit feedback on what's missing
- Consider whether Staff is achievable at current company or if you need to move

---

## Year 3 (May 2028 - May 2029): Transition to Engineering Manager

**Status**: You've made the switch to EM, or you're about to.

### Q1-Q2: First 6 Months as EM

**The Learning Curve**
- Your job is now your team's output, not your personal output
- You'll feel unproductive. This is normal.
- Resist the urge to code. Your job is to unblock others.

**Core Skills to Build**
- Running effective 1:1s (weekly, 30 min, their agenda)
- Giving feedback (specific, timely, actionable)
- Performance management (setting expectations, tracking progress)
- Hiring (writing job descriptions, running interviews, making decisions)

**Common Mistakes to Avoid**
- Solving technical problems yourself instead of coaching
- Avoiding difficult conversations
- Trying to be everyone's friend
- Micromanaging because you're anxious about output

### Q3-Q4: Finding Your EM Style

**What Kind of EM Are You?**
- Technical EM: Deep in architecture, light on process
- People EM: Strong on coaching, delegates technical decisions
- Process EM: Optimizes team operations, delivery predictability

All are valid. Know your strengths and compensate for weaknesses.

**Expanding Scope**
- Partner with PM on roadmap planning
- Represent your team in cross-functional meetings
- Build relationships with other EMs (you'll need allies)

---

## Year 4 (May 2029 - May 2030): Growing as EM

**Status**: You're a competent EM. Now you need to become a strong one.

### Q1-Q2: Handling Hard Situations

By now you should have encountered:
- A performance issue requiring a PIP or termination
- A team conflict you had to mediate
- A project that failed or was cancelled
- Losing a key engineer to another team or company

**How you handle these defines your reputation.**

### Q3-Q4: Positioning for Senior EM

**What Senior EM Requires**
- Managing a larger team (10-15 engineers) OR
- Managing multiple teams OR
- Managing a team with outsized impact/complexity

**Building the Case**
- Document your team's impact (metrics, shipped projects, business outcomes)
- Show you can handle scope beyond a single team
- Demonstrate you can coach other managers (even informally)

**Signals You're Ready**
- Other EMs ask you for advice
- Your skip-level (Senior EM or Director) gives you more autonomy
- You're involved in org-level decisions (hiring plans, team structure)

---

## Year 5 (May 2030 - May 2031): Senior EM → Director

**Status**: You're a Senior EM or about to be promoted.

### Q1-Q2: Operating as Senior EM

**Your Scope**
- 2-4 teams, 15-30 engineers
- You manage EMs, not just ICs
- You're responsible for a product area or technical domain

**New Skills**
- Coaching managers (different from coaching ICs)
- Org design (team structure, hiring plans, role definitions)
- Cross-functional leadership (partnering with Product, Design, Data leads)
- Strategic planning (quarterly and annual roadmaps)

### Q3-Q4: Building Director Case

**What Directors Do**
- Own outcomes for 30-50+ engineers across 3-6 teams
- Set technical and organizational strategy
- Represent engineering in executive discussions
- Make decisions that affect the entire org

**The Promotion**
- Directors are often hired, not promoted internally
- If your company has a path, you need executive sponsorship
- Your Senior EM or VP needs to advocate for you

**Alternative Path**
- Join a smaller company as Director
- Return to larger company at Director level later
- This is common and not a failure

---

## Key Milestones to Track

| Year | Target Level | Key Proof Points |
|------|--------------|------------------|
| 2 | Staff Engineer | Technical leadership across teams, design docs, mentorship |
| 3 | Engineering Manager | Team delivery, 1:1s, hiring, first performance review cycle |
| 4 | Strong EM | Handled hard situations, expanded scope, coaching others |
| 5 | Senior EM → Director | Managing managers, org-level impact, strategic planning |

---

## What Could Go Wrong

**Year 2: Staff promotion doesn't happen**
- Get explicit feedback. Is it timing, scope, or skill?
- Consider whether this company values what you bring
- Moving companies often accelerates promotions

**Year 3: You hate management**
- This is fine. Many people try EM and return to IC.
- Staff → Principal is a valid path to Director-level impact without managing people
- Better to learn this early than suffer for years

**Year 4: You're stuck as a mid-level EM**
- Common at large companies with many EMs
- Look for opportunities to take on more scope
- Consider smaller companies where you'd have more responsibility

**Year 5: Director role doesn't materialize**
- Director roles are scarce. There are fewer seats than qualified candidates.
- Consider VP of Engineering at a startup (same scope, different title)
- Patience matters. Some people reach Director at 35, some at 45.

---

## The Honest Truth

**This path takes 5-7 years if everything goes well.**

Most people don't follow a linear path. You might:
- Stay at Staff longer because you love the work
- Leave for a startup and skip Senior EM entirely
- Get laid off and have to rebuild momentum
- Realize Director isn't what you want after all

**The goal isn't to follow this plan perfectly. The goal is to keep growing.**

Check in annually:
1. Am I still learning?
2. Am I still excited about the next level?
3. Is this path still right for me?

If the answer to any of these is "no," it's time to reassess.
